In this manuscript we review and proof a remarkable
Binomial identity (5.43) given by Donald Knuth in
his fundamental work entitled
Concrete Mathematics, see~\cite[p. 190]{graham1994concrete}
\begin{proposition}[Knuth's Binomial identity (5.43)]
  \label{prop:knuth-identity}
  For non-negative integers $n$, and for arbitrary integers $s, r$
  \begin{align*}
    \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} \binom{r-sk}{n} (-1)^k = s^n.
  \end{align*}
\end{proposition}
